Childhood Days

As a child, I really loved being outdoors with my friends. We would gather almost every afternoon to play the popular games of the 90s. I remember playing langit-lupa, where we had to jump on higher ground to avoid getting tagged. We also played tumbang-preso, hitting the can with our slippers and running around excitedly.

Another favorite was patintero, where we blocked each other's ways with fast movements. Tagu-taguan, or a game of hide-and-seek, was always exciting because we had to find the best hiding spots. There was also the game of piko, which involved hopping with balance and laughter on drawn squares.

These games were more than fun; they taught us teamwork and creativity. Joy, energy, and endless afternoons were the contents of my childhood. The streets were our playground, and every day felt like an adventure.

Eventually, these games later transformed into real sports. I started to play basketball with passion, then I got interested in volleyball. These sports built up my foundation for discipline and teamwork. Now, looking back, I see how these childhood memories have built me up to who I am today.
